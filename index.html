The Challenge

Existing protocols weren't built for trust.

Existing synchronization protocols — NTP, TAI, UTC, GPS — and timestamp standards like ISO 8601 and RFC 3339 are widely used, yet susceptible to manipulation and inaccuracy. They rely on centralized sources, can't be independently verified, and lack a unified global standard.

Problem

Centralized time is unverifiable.

  • Reliance on centralized time servers creates single points of failure
  • No cryptographic proof that a timestamp hasn't been altered
  • Inconsistent standards across jurisdictions and systems
  • Significant risk in finance, legal, and supply-chain sectors
Solution

Clockchain makes time verifiable.

  • A decentralized validator network eliminates any single authority
  • Every timestamp is cryptographically signed and provable on-chain
  • A single global time standard usable by any system worldwide
  • A permanent, auditable ledger — no record can ever be altered
The Solution

Three primitives. One clock.

A decentralized physical network of certified time sources, aggregated by AI, validated on-chain through Proof-of-Time consensus.

AI + blockchain integration.

03

Proof-of-Time consensus.

A new consensus mechanism that aggregates time data from all global sources and validates it on-chain — ensuring accuracy and consistency across every block, every second.

PoT · On-chain validation
